We conclude that deploying the sentiment analysis strategy on the US drug manufacturer industry does not provide as accurate results as found by Isah et al (2018). The algorithm above achieves a win rate of 47%. However, with a 1.16 profit-loss ratio, it remains profitable throughout the 2020 stock market crash. Only after restricting trading to the most profitable day of the week (Berument & Kiymaz, 2001) does the strategy achieve profitability over this testing period. We attribute the decrease in performance to the commissions and spread costs simulated by LEAN.

To continue the development of this strategy, future areas of research include:

  • Adding a threshold parameter the cumulative sentiment counter must pass to signal trades
  • Only analyzing the sentiment of articles which contain keywords like "US, "USA", 'Q1', and others in their titles
  • Stemming and removing punctuation from articles before calculating their sentiment
  • Adding phrases to the sentiment dictionary or adjust the sentiment values
  • Adding other data feeds beside just Tiingo
